Manicure gardening services

Our manicure gardening services are designed to enhance the beauty and health of your outdoor spaces. We focus on detailed, high-quality care to ensure that every plant, flower, and feature looks its absolute best.

We provide thorough, ongoing maintenance to keep your garden in peak condition.

When to Trim:

Late Winter/Early Spring:
- Before new growth starts, which helps promote healthy growth.
After Blooming:
- For flowering shrubs that bloom on old wood, trim right after they bloom to avoid removing next season’s buds.

When to Trim Evergreen Shrubs:

Late Winter/Early Spring:
- Before new growth starts, which helps promote healthy growth.
Mid-Summer:
- For a light trim to shape and control size, avoiding heavy pruning.
- Regular light trimming maintains shape and density.

When to Trim Flowering Shrubs:

Immediately After Blooming:
- For shrubs that bloom on old wood, trimming after they flower ensures you don’t cut off next season’s blooms. Keep the plant in shape and promote more blooms.
Late Summer/Fall:
- For shrubs that bloom on new wood, pruning can be done in late summer to early fall.

When to Trim Fruit-bearing Shrubs:

Late Winter/Early Spring:
- Before new growth begins, helps improve air circulation and sunlight penetration.

When to Trim Hedge-like Shrubs:

Late Winter/Early Spring:
- For a formal hedge, trim before new growth starts.
Mid-Summer:
- Light trimming to maintain shape and density.

We remove the old trees:

When should you remove a stump:
- Tree stumps that are diseased or full of pests must be removed immediately.
- Old stumps may also attract certain pests and spread fungi or other diseases.
